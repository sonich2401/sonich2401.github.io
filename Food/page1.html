What is Food Insecurity?

Food insecurity is when someone is not sure where their next meal will come from. While food insecurity is a unfortunate situation for anyone, it has severely affected the children of our community that face food insecurity.

What is the deal with Food Insecurity?

Food insecurity can cause life long physical Health issues.

Food Insecurity is widespread in our nation and county

Food Insecurity can lead to worsened academic performance

How Big is Food Insecurity?

Lauren Bauer (2020), who holds a PhD in human development, finds that 18% of children are food-insecure (p. 2). Bauer (2020) also reports that our current state of food insecurity (as of 2020) for children is multiple times worse than it was in past major economic crises such as the Great Recession in 2008 (p. 2). 

Physical Effects

Food insecurity not only is annoying because your hungry, it also includes many downsides to overall health. Thomas, Miller, and Morrisse (2019) explain that food-insecure households have higher rates of lifetime medical issues and much higher rates of forgone medical care. (Thomas, Miller, and Morrisse, 2019, p. 1). It was also stated that a leading reason for the forgone medical care is that they cannot afford it (Thomas, Miller, and Morrisse, 2019, p. 4). Forgoing medical care is a dangerous path to go on and food-insecurity is not helping to avoid it.

Academic Effects

Not only does food-insecurity effect a child’s physical well-being, it also effects their ability to perform academically. Grineski, Morales, Collins, and Rubio (2018) explain the sad reality that children who lived in food-insecurity had significantly lower math scores than the children who live in food secure households (p. 7). Raising Whatcom County’s children with proper education will give all of us the peace of mind that our future is well educated and can make their way in the big world that we live in. However, food-insecurity hinders this goal.

What about Whatcom County

Could food insecurity be happening here in Whatcom County? Open Hands, a local food relief charity for school children , states that “41% of households are unable to afford basic needs” (Open Hands, n.d.
<= 2019, p. 1-2). This gives us a great clue as to the scale of the food-insecurity locally. I myself volunteered to help deliver food for Open Hands and I saw first hand how many bags of food were requested from each family. While I saw smiles on all children’s faces, I knew that inside a lot of them where hungry inside. Thankfully, Open Hands is not the only organization that is tackling food-insecurity in our county.
