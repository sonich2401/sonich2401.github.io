How will I conduct my Research?

To conduct my research, distributed a Google Forms survey with several questions that would give us some information to see how food-insecurity affects our local area. I also interviewed the leader for Open Hands Larae Tjoelker on how prevalent the problem of food-insecurity is in Whatcom County

(Figure 1)

How prevalent is it?

In this graph there is a large margin of respondents chose that they think about having enough food for the day often. This is much higher number than I expected.
The results from the survey showed that 31.6% of survey takers often think about finding enough food for the day (see figure 1). Bauer (2020) states, about 18% of children in the U.S. are not getting enough food to eat. (p. 2). Among the respondents the number of (nearly all of respondents were students) were significantly higher than the figure provided by Bauer (2020 p. 2). A similar number is also provided by authors Thomas, Miller, and Morrisse (2019) who gave the number of 13.9% (p. 3). In my interview with Larae Tjoelker(2021), when asked “Has the pendemic made the need for your organization greater or the same?”, she stated that more people did request for more food during the current pandemic.

(Figure 2)

How prevalent is it? (cont.)

In this question, 21.1% of respondents often though about choosing between buying food or other necessities. This means choosing between eating and other essential needs. This question is somewhat related to the question before this one however this helps identify the intensity of the food-insecurity that the respondent faced. Thomas, Miller, and Morrisse (2019) report that “... food-insecure households are more likely to have tight budget constraints and purchase cheaper, energy-dense foods” (p. 2). While the data from my survey does not show what quality of food choice the survey taker bought, it does give an idea to how much finances play into food-insecurity.
